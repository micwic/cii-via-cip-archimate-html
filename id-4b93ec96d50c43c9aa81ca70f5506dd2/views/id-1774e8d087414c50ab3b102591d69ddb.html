2.1.1 CII via CIP - Business Process (L1) ()
The prospective CII via CIP contracting party - the Renter Prosumer or the Owner Prosumer or the Property Owner - subscribes or unsubscribes to the CII via CIP service using the B2C portal. Possible business rules for service subscription : The Renter Prosumer tenant has a valid energy supply contract and the property owner has not already subscribed to CII via CIP service. The Owner Prosumer has a valid energy supply contract. The Poperty Owner has a valid grid connection contract and there is no CII for CIP subscribed for the point of consumption. Possible business rules for service unsubscription: Only the valid CII via CIP service contracting party may unsubscribe to the service. These different possible situations show that it is necessary to consider the contractual situation at the place of consumption and the connection object to authorize the activation and deactivation of the cii service via cip. Subscription to the CII via CIP service also requires acceptance of the general terms and conditions. See CII via CIP - Business Information Structure. The contracting party may download the authentication and encryption key using the B2C portal. When the property owner (or his representative/intermediary) subscribes to cii via cip service, this service cannot be managed by the tenant. In this case, too, the tenant's move will not result in any change to the state of the service. When a contractor who has subscribed to cii via cip service moves location (only Renter Prosumer or Owner Prosumer may moves), the service is in all circumstances technically deactivated on the associated smart meter. When the move results in a move within the grid to a consumption location with a compatible smart meter, the service is technically activated at the new location : variant a : with the key generated for the previous smart meter variant b : with a new generated key, without interrupting the validity of the service contract. As the service is not administratively interrupted, the key (initial or renewed) remains available for download from the B2C portal. When the move results in a move outside the grid, all the contract and associated subscriptions are terminated. When the smart meter needs to be replaced, for example in the event of a breakdown, the new smart meter is initialized with the configuration and with the key of the replaced smart meter. This does not cause any change of state in the business information related to the management of the cii via cip. Only the possible association of the physical smart meter to its logical representation is updated. The contractor may also request a service through the service desk. Only the subscription to CII via CIP was analyzed at this stage.
