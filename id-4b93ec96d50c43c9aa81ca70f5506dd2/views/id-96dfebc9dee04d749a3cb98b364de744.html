3.2.1.1 CII via CIP - Scenario WFM manually - Application Usage ()
In this scenario, the B2C portal allows customers to enter the necessary input data, generating a customer service request containing the relevant information required for the manual execution of the end-to-end process. The information entered by the customer is transmitted as-is; no verification or interaction is planned through this unidirectional channel. Tasks are distributed within the organization via the workflow management system of the customer relationship management system. These tasks are then presented to the appropriate internal stakeholders within their respective workflow management task bins. Tasks are executed manually using the applications of various systems: 1. The ERP system handles contractual situation checks, meter compatibility verification, and, if required, service subscription. 2. The HES manages the generation and retrieval of the authentication and encryption keys for CII via CIP, configures the smart meter to activate CII via CIP, and stores the key in an accessible location to ensure its secure transmission to the subscriber. The Customer Relationship Manager informs and/or gathers information from the customer requesting the service. During the initialization phase, this involves completing or correcting necessary information. After technical tasks are completed, the customer is informed that the service request has been fulfilled and can then download the key from the portal. In this scenario, customer interactions are strictly limited to subscribing to the CII via CIP service and downloading the key after successful completion of technical tasks. Interactions identified at the business process level through the portal but not supported in this scenario are indicated in red in the diagram.
