1.1 CII via CIP - Motivation Analysis ()
Making measurement data acquired by smart meters available online from the device itself is a legal obligation. This is usually done via the CIP port of smart meters in accordance with IDIS standards. The hardware and the data structure is usually defined according to the DSMR P1 standard and its derivatives. In principle, the port is activated on request (opt-in/out) and data transmitted are encrypted. A key must be generated and exchanged for installation on the customer system to authenticate to the smart meter and decrypt data. The legal obligation still only marginally implemented on the market, mainly for connecting home automation systems. By way of exception, electric vehicle charging stations and solar photovoltaic systems can also be connected to CIP port. We can deduce from these observations that the need for activation and deactivation is currently low on a very large number of installations. Although limited in number, the operations to be coordinated and carried out to manage the activation and deactivation option are relatively costly when carried out manually. These very specific operations are, however, similar to other opt-in/out services available through the intelligent measurement system or more generally within the company. There is a potential risk of an increase in the number of opt-in/out operations, and therefore of higher costs in the case of manual processing. The context of using CII via CIP on a residential smart meter can potentially involve a conversation with the owner or tenant of the consumption location. The contractual terms for the use of CII via CIP is generally managed by the ERP, while the technical activation/deactivation and generation of encryption keys is handled by the HES. This implies orchestration of the corresponding business processes and possible automations. These conditions argue in favor of orchestration with BPA-style automation with RPA.
