0.2 Architecture Vision ()
Beyond gaining maturity in Business Orchestration and Automation Tools technologies, the main objectives of the CII via CIP project are as follows: 1. Comply with the legal and regulatory framework of the Swiss Confederation's Energy Strategy 2050. 2. Enhance customer satisfaction by providing instant responses to legitimate requests. 3. Ensure operational efficiency by minimizing costs to the strict minimum, regardless of the number of events to be processed. This applies specifically to the scope of customer opt-in/out requests for access to Customer Information Interface (CII) services via the Customer Information Portal (CIP), whether such requests are submitted through the B2C Portal or received and processed directly by the Customer Service Request department. The project aims to assess the feasibility of fully automating the end-to-end execution of business processes, starting from the initial service request submitted by the customer via the corporate portal, and progressing in a controlled and traceable manner through all the necessary operations within both the ERP system and the administrative systems of the smart metering infrastructure. This includes the actual configuration of smart meters, the return of any required information, and continuous, real-time tracking of the process made available to the customer. In the current (as-is) business architecture, customer relations and operational processes are clearly separated. The processing of service requests requires coordination efforts (see trigger and information flow in the diagram)—partially supported by workflow management to reduce risks—yet still prone to latency, information loss, and other weaknesses inherent to this model. In the proposed target models, this organizational separation is removed in favor of a seamless end-to-end view within the DSO Customer Relationship Management business domain. This reflects the nature of the process: responding to a customer’s service request and delivering an authentication and encryption key after the required administrative and technical tasks have been completed. Solution Variants 2a and 2b aim to address these issues by drastically reducing human intervention in tasks that do not add value, while significantly enhancing the customer experience through faster outcomes and ongoing visibility into the process status. To a certain extent, some processing responsibilities are also shifted toward the customer, lightening the organizational load required to handle such requests. You can see in the diagram that DSO Internal Stakeholder are no more involved in these scenarios. Feasibility is being evaluated through the exploration of these three potential solution scenarios (1, 2a, and 2b)—each designed to fulfill the project's key objectives in the most efficient and effective way possible.
