Product Backlog with Product Roadmap The **Product Backlog** is the central list of work items required to create, improve, and maintain a product. It includes features, bug fixes, technical tasks, and research items that support the product’s overall development. Prioritized and maintained by the product owner, the backlog is a living document that evolves based on feedback and strategic shifts. The **Product Roadmap** is a high-level strategic document that outlines the planned evolution of the product over time. It defines major goals, themes, or features that the product team aims to deliver in the medium-to-long term. While the product backlog contains individual items, the roadmap provides the overarching direction and timeframe for these items. The roadmap is a guiding reference that informs backlog prioritization: - **Alignment of Backlog and Roadmap**: Items in the product backlog are often prioritized based on the roadmap. Features or capabilities listed on the roadmap for near-term releases are moved higher in priority, while long-term goals remain lower in the backlog until their designated timeframe approaches. - **Roadmap Themes and Epics**: Themes or epics from the roadmap typically flow into the product backlog as larger work items that represent significant pieces of functionality. These high-level items are later broken down into user stories or tasks as they approach the development cycle. - **Adapting to Change**: Both the roadmap and product backlog are flexible and may adjust based on new requirements, user feedback, or shifts in business strategy. While the roadmap provides a forecast of the product's direction, the backlog allows for detailed planning and adaptation within the framework provided by the roadmap. By integrating the roadmap with the product backlog, agile teams can prioritize tasks that align with strategic goals, ensuring that the product development remains on track with long-term objectives while remaining responsive to change. This alignment helps streamline decision-making and enables effective communication of progress to stakeholders.
