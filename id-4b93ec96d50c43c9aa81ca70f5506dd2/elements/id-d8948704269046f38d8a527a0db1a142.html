The **Sprint Backlog** is a detailed, short-term list of tasks and goals that the development team commits to completing within a sprint. In terms of data, the Sprint Backlog typically contains: - **Sprint Goal**: The primary objective or focus for the sprint, guiding the team’s work. - **Backlog Items (User Stories or Tasks)**: A list of items selected from the Product Backlog for this sprint, each including: - **ID**: Unique identifier for each item. - **Title**: Brief name or description of the item. - **Description**: Detailed explanation of the item, including purpose and acceptance criteria. - **Type**: Classification as a feature, bug, technical task, or improvement. - **Priority**: Level of importance of each item in the sprint. - **Status**: Current status (e.g., to-do, in-progress, done) to track progress. - **Effort Estimate**: Estimated effort required, often in hours or story points. - **Assigned Team Members**: Individuals responsible for the item. - **Dependencies**: Any other tasks or resources needed to complete the item. - **Subtasks**: Breakdown of individual backlog items into smaller, actionable tasks with: - **ID**: Unique identifier for each subtask. - **Description**: Brief description of the subtask. - **Effort Estimate**: Estimated time or effort for each subtask. - **Assigned Team Members**: Who is working on each subtask. - **Status**: Current progress of each subtask. - **Sprint Burndown Chart Data**: Tracking information for monitoring sprint progress, such as: - **Daily Remaining Effort**: Total estimated effort left to complete the sprint backlog each day. - **Trend Line**: Projected progress based on remaining effort, often displayed in a burndown chart. - **Issues/Risks**: Any identified risks or issues that may impact sprint completion, with: - **Risk/Issue Description**: Explanation of potential obstacles. - **Impact Level**: Assessment of how critical the issue is to the sprint goal. - **Mitigation Plans**: Actions planned to address or reduce the risk’s impact. This data in the Sprint Backlog allows the team to plan, track, and adjust their work during the sprint, ensuring they stay aligned with the sprint goal and can effectively measure progress.
