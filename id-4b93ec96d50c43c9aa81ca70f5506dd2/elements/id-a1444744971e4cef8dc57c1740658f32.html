The **Sprint Increment** is the sum of all completed Product Backlog items at the end of a sprint, representing a step toward achieving the final product. Each increment must be in a "done" state and meet the team’s Definition of Done, ensuring it’s potentially releasable and adds value to the product. In terms of data, the Sprint Increment typically includes: 1. **Completed Backlog Items**: All Product Backlog items finished during the sprint that contribute to the increment, each with: - **ID**: Unique identifier for tracking. - **Title and Description**: Brief name and details of each completed item. - **Acceptance Criteria**: Confirmation that criteria have been met and validated. - **Status**: Marked as “done” according to the Definition of Done. 2. **Definition of Done Compliance**: A checklist or confirmation that each backlog item meets all quality standards, including: - **Code Review and Testing**: Confirmation that code has been reviewed and tested. - **Documentation**: Any necessary documentation that accompanies the completed features. - **Approval**: Validation from relevant stakeholders that the item meets requirements. 3. **Integrated and Tested Code**: Code and components that have been integrated into the product’s codebase, ensuring: - **Continuous Integration Results**: Success in tests run on the integrated code. - **Version Control Information**: Versioning data confirming code integration. 4. **User Interface or Functional Changes**: Any visible or functional updates that enhance the product, with: - **Release Notes**: Summary of new features or updates. - **End-User Documentation**: Instructions or descriptions for users if applicable. 5. **Technical Metrics**: Information on technical quality, such as: - **Performance Metrics**: Data on speed, efficiency, or other performance changes. - **Bug Resolution**: List of bugs fixed and their impact on overall quality. 6. **Deployment Readiness**: Assurance that the increment is ready for potential deployment, including: - **Deployment Artifacts**: Packaged files, configurations, and necessary artifacts. - **Rollback Plan**: Procedures in case the deployment needs to be reversed. 7. **Sprint Review Report**: A summary document from the Sprint Review meeting, capturing stakeholder feedback and decisions made about the increment. It typically includes: - **Summary of the Increment**: Description of what was delivered, with a reminder of achieved sprint goals. - **Stakeholder Feedback**: Observations, suggestions, and critiques from stakeholders (product owner, clients, end users) regarding new features or improvements. - **Evaluation of Added Value**: Analysis of the increment's contribution to overall product goals. - **Priority Adjustments**: Any shifts or additions to Product Backlog priorities based on the feedback received. - **Decisions for Future Sprints**: Any items, adjustments, or features proposed for upcoming sprints based on review feedback. - **Issues and Improvement Areas**: Identification of any issues encountered during the sprint and suggestions for improving processes or product quality. 8. **Sprint Retrospective Report**: In the case of the CII via CIP PoC, the Sprint Retrospective is critical for continuous improvement. This report provides insights from the team on what went well, what could be improved, and actionable steps for future sprints. It typically includes: - **Team Reflections**: Observations on the sprint process, highlighting both positive aspects and challenges. - **Process Improvements**: Specific actions for enhancing team collaboration, communication, and efficiency. - **Technical Enhancements**: Suggestions for refining development practices, tooling, or workflow based on sprint experiences. - **Next Steps for Improvement**: Concrete changes the team will implement in upcoming sprints to address identified issues or leverage successful practices.
