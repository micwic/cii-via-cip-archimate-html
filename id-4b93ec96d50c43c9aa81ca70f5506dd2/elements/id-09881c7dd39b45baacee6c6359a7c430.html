CII via CIP PoC Business Architecture ()
### **CII via CIP PoC Business Architecture** The **CII via CIP Business Architecture** represents the business-level outcomes expected from the TOGAF **Architecture Vision phase**, specifically within the scope of the proof of concept (PoC) for the CII via CIP project. It defines the key business capabilities, processes, and roles required to meet the project objectives, as identified during the preliminary opportunity study. --- ### **Purpose** 1. **Alignment** - Establish a clear connection between business goals and the envisioned architecture. 2. **Scope Definition** - Provide a business-oriented framework for the proof of concept, ensuring alignment with regulatory, customer, and operational needs. 3. **Foundation for Detailed Design** - Serve as input for subsequent ADM phases (Business, Data, Application, and Technology Architectures). --- ### **Contents** 1. **Business Objectives** - Define the high-level goals that the CII via CIP architecture aims to support: - **Regulatory Compliance:** Ensure adherence to LApEl and relevant industry standards. - **Customer Empowerment:** Provide real-time access to energy consumption data via CIP. - **Operational Efficiency:** Streamline business processes for service activation and data handling. 2. **Key Capabilities** - Highlight the business capabilities required for the proof of concept: - **Data Access Management:** Enable secure and reliable customer access to metering data. - **Regulatory Reporting:** Meet legal requirements for data availability and transparency. - **Process Automation:** Automate key workflows such as service activation and deactivation. 3. **Business Processes** - Identify the high-level processes relevant to the proof of concept: - **Customer Request Handling:** Process customer opt-in/opt-out requests for CIP activation. - **Meter Data Access:** Enable data exchange between the smart meter, CIP, and customer portals. - **Compliance Monitoring:** Track and report regulatory compliance metrics. 4. **Roles and Stakeholders** - Define the business roles involved in the proof of concept: - **Business Owner:** Ensures alignment with strategic objectives and regulatory requirements. - **Customer Service:** Handles customer interactions and service requests. - **Regulatory Authority:** Monitors compliance with legal and industry standards. 5. **Scope of the Proof of Concept** - Define the boundaries of the PoC, limiting it to essential processes and capabilities required for validation: - Activation/deactivation of the CIP. - Data access for a subset of customers. - Preliminary compliance validation. --- ### **Lifecycle Role of the Data Object** 1. **Creation** - Developed during the **Architecture Vision phase** based on the opportunity study. 2. **Evolution** - Refined during later phases of the ADM as detailed architectures are developed. 3. **Use** - Guides the design and implementation of the proof of concept, ensuring alignment with business goals. --- ### **Benefits** - **Clarity:** Defines what the business expects from the CII via CIP architecture in the proof of concept. - **Traceability:** Links business objectives with architectural development. - **Scalability:** Provides a foundation for extending the business architecture beyond the proof of concept.
