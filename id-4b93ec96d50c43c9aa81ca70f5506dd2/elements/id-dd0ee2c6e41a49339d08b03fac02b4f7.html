### **Business Architect** In the **TOGAF framework**, a **Business Architect** is responsible for defining and documenting the **Business Architecture**, which includes the business strategy, governance, organization, and essential business processes. The Business Architect evaluates the organization's capabilities needed to fulfill its mission and ensures that business objectives are clearly articulated, providing the foundation for the design of supporting architectures (e.g., Data, Application, and Technology). ([pubs.opengroup.org](https://pubs.opengroup.org/architecture/togaf9-doc/arch/chap06.html)) In the **HERMES methodology**, the responsibilities often associated with a Business Architect are distributed between the **User Representatives** and the **Business Analyst**. The **User Representatives** focus on identifying and advocating for the organization's needs to achieve its mission, while the **Business Analyst** ensures that these needs are translated into actionable requirements. Together, they address the evaluation of capabilities and define how these capabilities should be realized within the project framework. ([hermes.admin.ch](https://www.hermes.admin.ch))
