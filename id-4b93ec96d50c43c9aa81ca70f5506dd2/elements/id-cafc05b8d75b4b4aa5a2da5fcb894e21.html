### **Steering Committee** The **Steering Committee** is a governance body responsible for providing strategic guidance, oversight, and decision-making for a project or program. It ensures alignment with organizational goals and oversees the progress, resources, and risks of the initiative. #### **Key Responsibilities**: 1. **Strategic Direction** - Define the project's strategic objectives and ensure alignment with organizational priorities. - Set the scope, goals, and high-level deliverables. 2. **Decision-Making** - Approve key project milestones and deliverables. - Resolve high-level issues and make critical decisions impacting the project's trajectory. 3. **Resource Oversight** - Allocate and monitor resources, including budget, personnel, and time. - Ensure efficient and effective use of organizational assets. 4. **Risk Management** - Identify and assess project risks, providing guidance for mitigation strategies. - Monitor compliance with organizational policies and regulatory requirements. 5. **Stakeholder Engagement** - Represent the interests of key stakeholders, ensuring their needs and concerns are addressed. - Communicate project status and outcomes to the broader organization. 6. **Performance Monitoring** - Review project progress against plans, milestones, and performance indicators. - Ensure accountability for results and adherence to timelines. #### **Composition of a Steering Committee (HERMES 5 Example)**: - **Business/Project Owner (Mandant)**: Leads the committee, providing overall authority and accountability for the project. - **User Representatives**: Represent the needs and expectations of the end-users or beneficiaries of the project. - **Producer Representatives**: Provide technical expertise and ensure feasibility and quality of deliverables. - **Operator Representatives**: Ensure the project aligns with operational and maintenance requirements post-implementation. #### **Importance**: The Steering Committee plays a critical role in balancing strategic oversight with operational execution, ensuring that the project delivers value and aligns with the organization's broader goals.
