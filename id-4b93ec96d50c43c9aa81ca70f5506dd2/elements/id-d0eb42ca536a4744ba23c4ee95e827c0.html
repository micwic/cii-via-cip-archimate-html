### **Solution Architecture Team Members** 1. **Solution Architect** - Leads the design of the CII via CIP architecture. - Ensures technical feasibility and alignment with standards (e.g., IDIS, DSMR P1). - Defines system components, interfaces, and integration points. - **Mandates specialists** to address technical, operational, and security-related questions, ensuring these aspects are thoroughly considered and integrated into the solution. 2. **Business Architect** - Identifies and evaluates the business capabilities required for the solution. - Ensures the architecture supports the organization's mission and strategic goals. - Collaborates with the Solution Architect to align the business and technical views. 3. **Business Owner** - Provides overall direction and ensures the solution delivers business value. - Represents the stakeholders and decision-makers responsible for funding and approving the solution. - Validates that business requirements are accurately reflected in the final design. --- ### **Specialists Potentially Mandated by the Solution Architect** 1. **Technical Specialist** - Provides expertise on the technologies being implemented (e.g., smart meters, CIP protocols). - Assists in detailed design, troubleshooting, and technology validation. 2. **Regulatory Compliance Specialist** - Ensures the solution complies with legal and regulatory frameworks (e.g., Swiss LApEl, ordinances). - Advises on standards and mitigates risks related to non-compliance. 3. **Infrastructure Specialist** - Designs the hosting environment and supports deployment. - Ensures scalability, security, and reliability. 4. **Security Specialist** - Assesses and mitigates security risks in the solution architecture. - Ensures compliance with cybersecurity standards and best practices. 5. **Tester/QA Specialist** - Validates that the solution meets business and technical requirements. - Performs integration and user acceptance testing. 6. **Change Manager** - Manages organizational impacts of implementing the CII via CIP solution. - Supports user adoption and transition to the new solution. --- ### **Conclusion** The current team (Solution Architect, Business Architect, and Business Owner) provides strong leadership in business and architectural strategy. By mandating specialists for technical, operational, and security aspects, the Solution Architect ensures comprehensive expertise is applied to critical areas, enabling a robust and compliant solution.
