In terms of data, the **Product Backlog** and **Product Roadmap** contain structured and prioritized information to guide the development team. Here’s a breakdown of each: ### Product Backlog The Product Backlog holds a detailed list of items, typically structured with the following data: - **ID**: Unique identifier for each backlog item. - **Title**: Short descriptive name of the item. - **Description**: Detailed explanation of the item’s purpose, requirements, and acceptance criteria. - **Type**: Classification of the item, such as feature, bug, task, or technical improvement. - **Priority**: Level of importance, usually defined by the product owner, to help with ordering items. - **Status**: Current progress stage (e.g., to-do, in-progress, completed). - **Estimated Effort**: Effort estimate (in hours, story points, etc.) to complete the item. - **Dependencies**: Other backlog items or external factors that must be addressed before this item. - **Assigned Team Members**: Individuals or teams responsible for the item. - **Tags or Labels**: Keywords to help categorize or filter items (e.g., 'UI', 'backend', 'critical'). ### Product Roadmap The Product Roadmap is a higher-level, strategic document that outlines the product's goals and timeline. It contains data points such as: - **Themes/Epics**: Major areas of focus, representing significant features or product goals. - **Objectives**: Strategic goals or outcomes that the roadmap is intended to achieve. - **Milestones**: Key dates or phases (e.g., Alpha, Beta, General Availability) for planned deliveries. - **Timeline**: Estimated timeframes for achieving each milestone or completing each theme/epic. - **Priority**: Relative importance of each roadmap item to guide prioritization and sequencing. - **Dependencies and Risks**: Major dependencies or potential risks associated with each roadmap item. - **Progress Status**: Current state of each item (e.g., planning, in-progress, completed). - **Stakeholders**: Key individuals or groups interested in or affected by the roadmap item. - **Metrics**: Key performance indicators (KPIs) or metrics to measure progress and success. Together, these data points in the Product Backlog and Product Roadmap provide a comprehensive view of what needs to be built, how it aligns with strategic goals, and how the team can prioritize and track progress effectively.
